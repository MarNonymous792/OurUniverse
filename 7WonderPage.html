Seven Wonders of the World

Explore the greatest architectural and engineering achievements of ancient and modern times

Ancient Wonders

Ancient Egypt

Great Pyramid of Giza

Location: Giza, Egypt

Built: c. 2589–2566 BCE

Built For: Pharaoh Khufu

Original Height: 146.5 meters

Known For: The only surviving wonder of the ancient world constructed with 2.3 million limestone blocks.

Built as an elaborate tomb for Pharaoh Khufu of the Fourth Dynasty, the Great Pyramid represents one of humanity's greatest architectural achievements. Originally standing at 146.5 meters tall and covered in smooth white limestone, it reflects the remarkable precision and engineering capabilities of ancient Egypt. It remains the only surviving wonder of the ancient world.

Ancient Babylon

Hanging Gardens

Location: Babylon, Iraq

Built: c. 610–562 BCE

Built By: King Nebuchadnezzar II

Status: Legendary; no conclusive archaeological evidence

Known For: Terraced gardens with exotic plants and innovative irrigation systems.

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon are renowned in ancient literature as a marvel of engineering and horticulture. According to legend, they were built by King Nebuchadnezzar II to comfort his wife, Amytis, who missed the green hills of her homeland. The gardens featured multiple terraced levels filled with exotic plants, connected by vaulted arches.

Ancient Greece

Temple of Artemis

Location: Ephesus, Turkey

Built: c. 550 BCE

Architect: Chersiphron and Metagenes

Dimensions: 115 meters long, 55 meters wide

Known For: Magnificent Ionic temple with 127 marble columns and sculptural decorations.

The Temple of Artemis in Ephesus was one of the largest structures of the ancient world. Dedicated to the Greek goddess of the hunt, this magnificent temple featured 127 marble columns arranged in a double row. The temple was renowned for its intricate sculptures and artistic decorations.

Ancient Greece

Statue of Zeus

Location: Olympia, Greece

Created: c. 435 BCE

Sculptor: Phidias

Height: 12 meters (40 feet)

Materials: Ivory, gold, ebony, and precious stones

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was a monumental chryselephantine (gold and ivory) sculpture created by the renowned sculptor Phidias around 435 BCE. Standing approximately 12 meters tall, the statue was one of the most celebrated works of art in the ancient world. It depicted the king of the gods seated on a magnificent throne adorned with jewels and carvings.

Ancient Turkey

Mausoleum

Location: Halicarnassus, Turkey

Built: c. 353 BCE

Built For: Mausolus, governor of Caria

Height: Approximately 45 meters

Known For: Ornate marble tomb that gave the world the term "mausoleum".

The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus was an elaborate marble tomb built around 353 BCE to honor Mausolus, the governor of Caria. Designed by the Greek architects Satyros and Pythius, this monumental structure stood approximately 45 meters tall and featured intricate sculptural decorations. The term "mausoleum" itself derives from this magnificent tomb.

Ancient Greece

Colossus of Rhodes

Location: Rhodes, Greece

Built: c. 292–280 BCE

Sculptor: Chares of Lindos

Height: Approximately 33 meters (108 feet)

Known For: Massive bronze statue of sun god Helios, symbol of freedom and protection.

The Colossus of Rhodes was a towering bronze statue of the sun god Helios, erected around 280 BCE to celebrate Rhodes' victory over a besieging army. Created by sculptor Chares of Lindos, this monumental statue stood approximately 33 meters tall and was crafted from bronze plates riveted together. It was one of the tallest statues of the ancient world.

Ancient Egypt

Lighthouse of Alexandria

Location: Alexandria, Egypt

Built: c. 280 BCE

Built For: Ptolemy II

Height: Approximately 100 meters (330 feet)

Known For: Ancient lighthouse (Pharos) that guided ships and was visible up to 50 kilometers away.

The Lighthouse of Alexandria, also known as the Pharos, was one of the tallest man-made structures of the ancient world. Built around 280 BCE during the reign of Ptolemy II, this magnificent lighthouse stood on the island of Pharos and used mirrors and fire to guide ships safely into the harbor. It remained operational for nearly 1,500 years.

Modern Wonders

Modern China

Great Wall of China

Location: China

Length: Approximately 21,196 kilometers

Built Over: More than 2,000 years (multiple dynasties)

Purpose: Military defense and border control

Known For: One of the most impressive architectural feats, visible from space.

The Great Wall of China is one of the most iconic structures in the world, stretching across northern China. Built over more than 2,000 years by multiple dynasties, it served as a military defense system and border control mechanism. The wall was constructed to protect against invasions from northern tribes and to control trade along the Silk Road.

Modern India

Taj Mahal

Location: Agra, India

Built: 1632–1653

Built By: Emperor Shah Jahan

Built For: His wife Mumtaz Mahal

Known For: Monument to eternal love, featuring exquisite white marble and intricate inlay work.

The Taj Mahal is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world. Built between 1632 and 1653 by Emperor Shah Jahan as a mausoleum for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, this magnificent structure is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. The Taj Mahal features exquisite white marble inlaid with semi-precious stones.

Modern Brazil

Christ the Redeemer

Location: Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Built: 1922–1931

Height: 38 meters (125 feet)

Design: Art Deco style

Known For: Iconic symbol of Rio, overlooking from atop Corcovado Mountain.

Christ the Redeemer is a colossal Art Deco statue overlooking Rio de Janeiro from Corcovado Mountain. Built between 1922 and 1931, this iconic monument stands 38 meters tall and has become a symbol of Brazilian culture. The statue depicts Jesus Christ with arms outstretched in blessing and protection over the city.

Modern Peru

Machu Picchu

Location: Cusco Region, Peru

Built: c. 1450

Built By: Inca Empire

Altitude: 2,430 meters above sea level

Known For: Ancient Incan citadel showcasing remarkable engineering and stone masonry.

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan citadel situated on a mountain ridge high in the Andes. Built around 1450, this remarkable site showcases incredible engineering and architectural prowess. The structures were built without mortar, using precisely cut stones that fit together perfectly.

Modern Mexico

Chichen Itza

Location: Yucatan, Mexico

Founded: c. 600 CE

Period: Mayan and Toltec

Famous Structure: El Castillo (The Castle pyramid)

Known For: Blending Mayan and Toltec architectural styles.

Chichen Itza is one of the largest pre-Columbian archaeological sites in Mexico. Founded around 600 CE, this site represents a fascinating blend of Mayan and Toltec architectural styles. The most iconic structure is El Castillo (The Castle), a large pyramid that serves as the centerpiece of the complex.

Modern Italy

Roman Colosseum

Location: Rome, Italy

Built: 72–80 CE

Built By: Roman Emperors Vespasian and Titus

Capacity: Approximately 50,000–80,000 spectators

Known For: Ancient amphitheater hosting gladiatorial contests and public spectacles.

The Roman Colosseum is an iconic symbol of Imperial Rome and one of the most impressive surviving structures from the ancient world. Built between 72 and 80 CE, this massive amphitheater could hold up to 80,000 spectators. It was used for gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, and public spectacles.

Modern Jordan

Petra

Location: Ma'an Governorate, Jordan

Carved: c. 100 BCE – 100 CE

Built By: Nabataean Kingdom

Stone: Rose-red sandstone

Known For: Ancient city carved into rock cliffs, major trading hub.

Petra is a magnificent ancient city carved into rose-red sandstone cliffs in Jordan. Established by the Nabataean Kingdom around 100 BCE, it served as a major trading hub. The city is famous for its intricate rock-cut architecture and sophisticated water management system.
