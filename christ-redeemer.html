Christ the Redeemer

Brazil

Modern Wonder

Overview

Christ the Redeemer is a colossal statue of Jesus Christ located atop Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. It is one of the most recognizable statues in the world and has become an iconic symbol of Brazil and Christianity.

Construction and Design

The statue was designed by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, French-Polish sculptor Paul Landowski, and French engineer Albert Caquot. Construction began in 1922 and was completed in 1931, taking approximately 9 years to finish.

Impressive Dimensions

Christ the Redeemer stands 30 meters (98 feet) tall, with an additional 8 meters (26 feet) for the pedestal, bringing the total height to 38 meters (125 feet). The statue weighs approximately 635 tons and has an arm span of 28 meters (92 feet).

Location and Accessibility

Perched on top of Mount Corcovado at 709 meters (2,326 feet) above sea level, the statue offers spectacular views of Rio de Janeiro. Visitors can reach it by train, van, or hiking trail, making it one of the most visited monuments in the world.

Religious Significance

Christ the Redeemer is not only an architectural marvel but also a powerful religious symbol. The statue's outstretched arms seem to embrace the city below, symbolizing protection and divine blessing for Rio de Janeiro and its people.

Global Icon

The statue was officially declared one of the New Seven Wonders of the World in 2007. Today, it attracts approximately 2 million visitors annually and remains one of the most photographed monuments in the world.
