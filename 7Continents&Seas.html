7 Continents & 5 Oceans

Explore the Earth's massive landmasses and vast waters

The World at a Glance

The Earth is divided into seven large landmasses known as "Continents" and five major bodies of saltwater known as "Oceans". These divisions help us understand the geography, climate, and biodiversity of our planet.

The 7 Continents

Largest Continent

Asia

Area: ~44.5 million km²

Population: ~4.7 billion (Most populous)

Major Countries: China, India, Japan, Russia

Known For: Mount Everest (highest point) and the Dead Sea (lowest point).

Asia is the largest and most populous continent, covering about 30% of Earth's total land area. It shares the landmass of Eurasia with Europe but is culturally and geographically distinct.

It is the birthplace of many major religions, including Buddhism, Christianity, Hinduism, Islam, and Judaism. Its geography varies wildly from the frozen tundra of Siberia to the tropical jungles of Indonesia.

The Mother Continent

Africa

Area: ~30.3 million km²

Population: ~1.4 billion

Countries: 54 (Most of any continent)

Known For: The Nile River, Sahara Desert, and diverse wildlife.

Africa is the second-largest and second-most-populous continent. It is widely regarded as the "cradle of humankind," where the earliest humans evolved.

It is the only continent to stretch from the northern temperate to southern temperate zones. It houses the world's longest river (the Nile) and the world's largest hot desert (the Sahara).

The New World

North America

Area: ~24.7 million km²

Population: ~592 million

Major Countries: USA, Canada, Mexico

Known For: The Great Lakes, Grand Canyon, and Greenland.

North America is the third-largest continent. It contains every major biome, from the frozen Arctic in the north to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It is home to the largest island in the world (Greenland) and the largest freshwater lake system (the Great Lakes).

Home of the Amazon

South America

Area: ~17.8 million km²

Population: ~430 million

Major Countries: Brazil, Argentina, Colombia

Known For: Amazon Rainforest, Andes Mountains, Angel Falls.

South America is the fourth-largest continent. It is dominated by the Amazon Rainforest, often called the "Lungs of the Earth," and the Andes, the world's longest continental mountain range.

The Frozen Continent

Antarctica

Area: ~14.2 million km²

Population: ~1,000–5,000 (Scientists only)

Climate: Coldest, driest, and windiest continent

Known For: Penguins and holding 90% of Earth's ice.

Antarctica is the fifth-largest continent and is almost entirely covered in ice. It has no permanent residents, only visiting researchers.

Because it receives very little precipitation, it is technically the largest desert in the world.

The Peninsula of Peninsulas

Europe

Area: ~10.2 million km²

Population: ~746 million

Major Countries: Germany, UK, France, Italy

Known For: Rich history, the Vatican (smallest country), and diverse cultures.

Europe is the second-smallest continent by surface area. It is separated from Asia by the Ural Mountains.

Despite its small size, it has had a profound influence on global history, art, and science.

The Island Continent

Australia

Area: ~8.6 million km²

Population: ~43 million

Composition: Mainland Australia, New Zealand, Pacific Islands

Known For: Great Barrier Reef, Kangaroos, and Koalas.

Australia (often grouped with Oceania) is the smallest continent. It is the lowest, flattest, and oldest landmass on Earth.

Because of its isolation, it has developed unique wildlife found nowhere else on the planet.

The 5 Oceans

The Largest Ocean

Pacific Ocean

Size: 165.2 million km²

Depth: Deepest ocean (Mariana Trench)

Coverage: ~30% of Earth's surface

Known For: Ring of Fire (volcanoes) and immense size.

The Pacific Ocean is the largest and deepest of Earth's oceanic divisions. It is larger than all of Earth's landmasses combined.

Its name was coined by explorer Ferdinand Magellan, meaning "peaceful sea," though it is home to frequent typhoons and volcanic activity.

The S-Shaped Ocean

Atlantic Ocean

Size: 106.5 million km²

Rank: 2nd Largest

Salinity: Saltiest major ocean

Known For: Separating the "Old World" from the "New World".

The Atlantic Ocean separates the Americas from Europe and Africa. It is known for its distinct "S" shape.

The Warmest Ocean

Indian Ocean

Size: 70.6 million km²

Rank: 3rd Largest

Location: Between Africa, Asia, and Australia

Known For: Historic spice trade routes.

The Indian Ocean is the warmest ocean in the world. It has played a crucial role in history as a major trade route connecting East and West.

The Newest Ocean

Southern Ocean

Size: 20.3 million km²

Status: Officially recognized in 2000

Location: Surrounds Antarctica

Known For: Circumpolar current and extreme storms.

The Southern Ocean (also known as the Antarctic Ocean) is the newest named ocean. It completely encircles Antarctica and is defined by currents rather than land borders.

The Shallowest Ocean

Arctic Ocean

Size: 14.06 million km²

Rank: Smallest Ocean

Location: North Polar Region

Known For: Polar bears and sea ice.

The Arctic Ocean is the smallest and shallowest of the world's five major oceans. It is mostly covered by sea ice throughout the year.
