Taj Mahal

India

Modern Wonder

Overview

The Taj Mahal is widely considered one of the most beautiful buildings in the world. Located in Agra, India, it is a white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as a tribute to his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

A Monument to Love

Built between 1632 and 1653, the Taj Mahal was constructed following the death of Mumtaz Mahal during childbirth. Shah Jahan commissioned this architectural masterpiece to honor his wife's memory and their enduring love.

Architectural Design

The Taj Mahal was designed by a team of architects including Ustad Ahmad Lahauri. The structure features a perfect blend of Mughal and Persian architectural styles, with a symmetrical design that is visually stunning from every angle.

Construction and Materials

The monument is constructed entirely of white marble inlaid with semi-precious stones such as jade, crystal, turquoise, and coral. The marble was quarried from Makrana in Rajasthan, approximately 370 kilometers away, and transported to the construction site.

Intricate Details

Every aspect of the Taj Mahal showcases exquisite craftsmanship. The walls are adorned with intricate floral inlays, calligraphy, and precious stones. The dome and minarets are perfect examples of Mughal architectural precision and artistry.

UNESCO World Heritage Site

The Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1972. It attracts millions of visitors annually and is considered one of the most iconic symbols of love, beauty, and artistic achievement in human history.
